Abhinav Sinha was born in India. He obtained a Bachelor of Technology (B. Later, he was nominated as a visiting faculty at TCS Global Learning Center for talent development in engineering and industrial services and role of IT in manufacturing.

He is a recipient of TCS On the Spot Award for his services and contributions towards the company, and TCS Champions of Initial Learning Programme Award for his contributions as a faculty. He has also received IEEE best paper award by IEEE Computer Society and IEEE Pune Section. To pursue a full-time career in research and academia, he joined Indian Institute of Engineering Science and Technology for a master’s course in Mechatronics and Robotics. After completing coursework at IIEST, he is currently associated with Central Scientific Instruments Organization as post graduate researcher working towards completion of his dissertation.

Abhinav is a passionate towards applied mathematics, systems and control theory, teaching and research. When not formulating control protocols and educating young students, he can be found enjoying his time with family and friends, reading poetry and fiction, solving sudoku, travelling, watching movies and playing Age of Empires. Continuously exploring new realms of learning, he wishes many more shall join him in his endeavour.

Thesis: Automated PID tuning of line follower robot

This project report on automated PID tuning of line follower robots describes an effective way of controlling line following robots, i.e., a category of mobile robots via a simple yet effective control methodology based on classic Proportional-Integral-Derivative control. The conventional way of running, testing and implementing line follower robots is based on providing certain conditions to the robot in the form of a computer program and it has to move according to the conditions provided for different paths. However, this does not make the robot capable of taking decisions such as how to overcome the wobbling and to reduce the error that may creep in the form of deviation from the current position or say, overshoot from the line. To achieve the objective of enabling the robot to correct these moves on its own or to enable the robot to control and reduce the overshoot so as to stay on course, providing smoothness, we have implemented Proportional-Integral-Derivative control based on the informations that can be gathered from the movement of the robot.

Advisor: Prof. Rajiv Kumar Mishra
